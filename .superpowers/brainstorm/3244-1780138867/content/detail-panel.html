A

Tabbed Panel (Recommended)

Horizontal tab bar at top of detail panel. One tab per artifact. Content area switches. Current selection is clearly indicated.

Pros

  • Clear navigation — user always knows what they're looking at
  • One artifact visible at a time = focused reading
  • Familiar pattern (browser tabs, IDE panels)
  • Works well with markdown rendered content

Cons

  • Can't compare two artifacts side by side
  • Tab bar takes vertical space
B

Accordion Sections

Collapsible sections stacked vertically. User expands what's relevant. Multiple sections can be open simultaneously.

Pros

  • Can compare sections side by side
  • Saves space when collapsed
  • Good for browsing multiple artifacts

Cons

  • Expanding/collapsing is slow for frequent switching
  • Scrolling through all four open gets long
  • Less discoverable than tabs
